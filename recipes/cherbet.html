Cherbet Algerienne Recipe

Home

Description

Cherbet Algerienne - meaning literally Algerian Cherbet is an Algerian lemonade with an unusual twist - milk. This may be one of the most underrated drinks on planet earth, so strap up, squeeze your lemons and stick to the end.

What you'll need

Tools

  • Lemon Juicer
  • Zester / Grater
  • Saucepan
  • Spoon
  • Bottle for storage
  • Sieve / Filter

Ingredients

  • 350ml lemon juice (~5 lemons)
  • 2 tablespoons of orange blossom water
  • 5 tablespoons of sugar
  • 4 tablespoons of milk
  • 1.5l of boiling water

Steps

  1. Cut a lemon into thin slices.
  2. Boil half a liter of water.
  3. Place the lemon slices in a heatproof pitcher or bottle of your choice.
  4. Pour boiling water over the mixture and let it steep completely until cool.
  5. Once cold, squeeze the juice of the 4 remaining lemons. Add to the mixture.
  6. Add 5 tablespoons of sugar to the mixture. (adjust to taste)
  7. Pour in 2 tablespoons of orange blossom water.
  8. Stir in 4 tablespoons of milk. Make sure it mixes in well.
  9. Add the remaining liter of water and mix well in order to dissolve the remaining sugar.
  10. Store in a clean bottle. Store in a cool area, preferably in your fridge.
  11. Served cold. Enjoy!
